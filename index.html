My Profile

I began my professional journey at Kuwait Oil Company (KOC) as a Health Information Management Systems (HIMS) Officer stationed at Ahmadi Hospital, where I worked in the procurement unit. During my seven months of experience before being conscripted into the military, I gained extensive hands-on exposure to procurement processes, particularly in managing contracts for acquiring technology-related equipment and services. My role required collaborating with vendors, reviewing contracts, and ensuring the timely procurement of essential items critical to the hospital’s operations. Working in the procurement unit, I handled both routine purchases and large-scale contracts with significant impact on the hospital as a whole. This included sourcing high-value, technology-focused solutions to support various departments. I developed a keen understanding of balancing technical requirements, budgetary constraints, and operational timelines, ensuring optimal outcomes for all stakeholders involved. This role provided me with valuable insights into the complexities of procurement within a healthcare setting and helped refine my skills in communication, negotiation, and project coordination. It was a dynamic and challenging environment that allowed me to make meaningful contributions to the hospital’s success. This experience has been instrumental in shaping my professional development and fostering a strong foundation for tackling future challenges.

During my mandatory military service, I underwent two months of intensive training focused on traditional military skills, including physical fitness, weapon handling, and understanding formal military culture. This period provided me with a strong foundation in discipline, teamwork, and resilience—qualities essential to both military and professional life. After completing the initial training, I was assigned to the Directorate of Cyber Operations, where I served for 10 months. This assignment allowed me to engage in various cybersecurity-focused roles and work across multiple units. My responsibilities included penetration testing to identify and mitigate system vulnerabilities, malware analysis to understand and combat potential threats, and threat hunting to proactively identify risks before they could impact critical systems. Additionally, I contributed to programming tasks and provided technical support, ensuring that operational technology remained secure and functional. My time in the Directorate of Cyber Operations was both challenging and rewarding, offering hands-on experience in advanced cybersecurity practices. It deepened my understanding of modern cyber threats and the measures required to address them. This experience not only enhanced my technical skills but also strengthened my ability to think critically and adapt to rapidly changing scenarios, qualities that I continue to apply in my professional endeavors.
